Quick FAQs
Can a landlord evict without notice? ▼
No. Under the Punjab Rented Premises Act 2009, a landlord must give written notice and get a Rent Controller court order. Forceful eviction without a court order is illegal and punishable.
How is property inherited in Pakistan? ▼
Under the Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act 1962, sons receive double the share of daughters. Spouses, parents, and children all have fixed Quranic shares enforced by Pakistani courts.
Is a verbal rental agreement valid? ▼
It may be valid under the Contract Act 1872 but is nearly impossible to prove. Always get a written signed agreement registered with the local union council for full legal protection.

Quick FAQs
Can a woman get Khula? ▼
Yes. Under Section 8 of the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance 1961, a woman can apply for Khula in Family Court without her husband's consent. Process takes 3-6 months.
Who gets custody of children? ▼
Under the Guardian and Wards Act 1890, the mother has hizanat (custody) of sons until age 7 and daughters until puberty. Courts always prioritize the child's best interest.
Is mehr mandatory? ▼
Yes. Mehr is mandatory under the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance 1961. It must be stated in the Nikahnama and the wife can claim it at any point during or after marriage.

Quick FAQs
Can police arrest without warrant? ▼
Under Section 54 CrPC, police can arrest without warrant only when catching someone mid-crime, for serious cognizable offence suspects, or prior criminal record holders. All other arrests require a magistrate warrant.
Rights when arrested? ▼
Under Article 10 of the Constitution: right to know reason for arrest, right to a lawyer immediately, right to be produced before a magistrate within 24 hours. Torture violates Article 14 and is a criminal offence.
Police refuse to file FIR? ▼
Under Section 154 CrPC, the SHO is legally bound to register an FIR. If refused: approach the SP, file a complaint in Magistrate Court under Section 200 CrPC. Refusal is itself punishable.

Quick FAQs
Minimum wage in Pakistan? ▼
Under the Minimum Wages Ordinance 1961, the federal minimum wage is PKR 37,000/month as of 2024. Paying below this is a criminal offence. File a free complaint at the Labour Department.
Can I be fired without notice? ▼
No. Under the Industrial and Commercial Employment Ordinance 1968, one month written notice or pay in lieu is required. Termination without cause entitles you to compensation through the Labour Court.
Working hours limit? ▼
Under the Factories Act 1934, maximum 8 hours/day and 48 hours/week. Any work beyond this must be paid as overtime at double the normal rate under Section 34 of the Act.

Quick FAQs
How to register a business? ▼
Under the Companies Act 2017, register at secp.gov.pk online. For sole proprietorship, get NTN from FBR at fbr.gov.pk. Company registration takes 1-3 days and costs PKR 1,500-3,000.
Is a verbal contract binding? ▼
Yes under the Contract Act 1872 (Section 10), but nearly impossible to prove. Always use a written agreement — even a simple signed document is fully enforceable in Pakistani civil court.
Cheque bounce — what to do? ▼
Under Section 489-F PPC, issuing a dishonoured cheque is a criminal offence punishable up to 3 years. File a criminal complaint at the police station and a civil recovery suit simultaneously.

Quick FAQs
Key constitutional rights? ▼
Art 9: Right to life. Art 10: Protection from arbitrary arrest. Art 10-A: Right to fair trial. Art 14: Dignity and no torture. Art 19: Free speech. Art 20: Freedom of religion. Art 25: Equality. Art 25-A: Free education ages 5-16.
What is a writ petition? ▼
Under Article 199 of the Constitution, any citizen can file a writ petition in the High Court when fundamental rights are violated. The court can immediately direct any authority to stop the violation.
Is free education a right? ▼
Yes. Article 25-A (added by 18th Amendment 2010) makes free and compulsory education a fundamental right for children aged 5-16. If denied, a writ petition can be filed in the High Court.
