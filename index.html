Quick FAQs
Can police arrest without warrant? ▼
Under the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), police may arrest without warrant in cognizable offences caught in progress. For other matters, a magistrate's warrant is required. If arrested, you must be produced before a magistrate within 24 hours.
What if police refuse FIR? ▼
The SHO is legally bound to register your FIR for cognizable offences. If refused, escalate to the DSP or SP. You may also file a direct complaint before the Area Magistrate under the CrPC.

Quick FAQs
Can a woman get Khula? ▼
Yes. Under the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance 1961, a woman may seek Khula through the Family Court. The court attempts reconciliation. If unsuccessful, Khula is granted. The wife may need to return the mehr.
Who gets child custody? ▼
Under the Guardian and Wards Act 1890, courts determine custody based on the child's best interest. Mothers traditionally retain custody of young children, but courts may differ based on circumstances.

Quick FAQs
Can landlord evict without notice? ▼
No. Under provincial Rent Control legislation, forcible eviction without a Rent Tribunal court order is illegal. The landlord must follow due legal process.
What is Fard-e-Malkiat? ▼
Fard-e-Malkiat is the official land ownership record from the Revenue Department. It is the primary document proving land ownership. Always verify before any property transaction.

Quick FAQs
Where to report cybercrime? ▼
Report to the FIA Cyber Crime Reporting Centre (CCRC) at fia.gov.pk or visit any CCRC office. Keep all digital evidence — screenshots, links, phone numbers — before reporting.
What law covers cybercrime? ▼
The Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act 2016 (PECA 2016) covers online harassment, hacking, digital blackmail, fake content, and cyber fraud. The FIA is the investigating authority.

Quick FAQs
Minimum wage in Pakistan? ▼
The federal minimum wage is PKR 37,000/month as of 2024 under the Minimum Wages Ordinance 1961. Violations are reported to the district Labour Department — filing is free.
Can I be fired without notice? ▼
Under the Industrial and Commercial Employment Ordinance 1968, one month's written notice or equivalent pay is required. Wrongful termination can be challenged before the Labour Court.

Quick FAQs
How to register a company? ▼
Register with SECP under the Companies Act 2017 at secp.gov.pk. For sole proprietorship, get NTN from FBR at fbr.gov.pk. Takes 1-3 days.
Cheque bounce — what to do? ▼
A dishonoured cheque is a criminal offence under the Pakistan Penal Code. File a criminal complaint at the police station and a civil recovery suit simultaneously.
