Quick FAQs
Can police arrest without warrant? ▼
Under the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), police may arrest without warrant in cognizable offences — serious crimes like theft, assault, or murder caught in progress. For non-cognizable matters, a magistrate's warrant is required. If arrested, you must be produced before a magistrate within 24 hours.
What if police refuse FIR? ▼
Under the CrPC, the SHO is legally bound to register your FIR for cognizable offences. If refused, escalate to the DSP or SP. You may also file a direct complaint before the Area Magistrate. Unjustified refusal is itself a punishable act.
Bailable vs non-bailable offence? ▼
Bailable offences allow bail as a right upon request. Non-bailable offences (such as murder or robbery) require a court to grant bail based on case merits. A Sessions Court handles pre-arrest bail applications for serious matters.

Quick FAQs
Can a woman get Khula? ▼
Yes. Under the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance 1961, a woman may seek Khula through the Family Court. The court summons both parties and attempts reconciliation. If unsuccessful, Khula is granted. The wife may be required to return the mehr. The process typically takes several months.
Who gets custody of children? ▼
Under the Guardian and Wards Act 1890, courts determine custody based primarily on the child's best interest. Traditionally, mothers retain custody of young children (sons until age 7, daughters until puberty), but courts may differ based on circumstances.
Is mehr mandatory? ▼
Yes. Mehr is a mandatory right of the wife under Islamic law and the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance 1961. It must be specified in the Nikahnama. The wife may claim it at any point — during the marriage or upon divorce.

Quick FAQs
Can landlord evict without notice? ▼
No. Under provincial Rent Control legislation (such as the Punjab Rented Premises Act), forcible eviction without a Rent Tribunal court order is illegal. The landlord must follow due process — notice, then court proceedings.
How is property inherited in Pakistan? ▼
For Muslims, inheritance follows the Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act. Shares are fixed by Quranic principles — sons generally receive double the share of daughters. The local Civil Court or Revenue Office handles inheritance matters.
What is Fard-e-Malkiat? ▼
Fard-e-Malkiat is an official land ownership record issued by the Revenue Department (Patwari office). It is the primary document proving ownership of agricultural or residential land. Always verify this before any property transaction.

Quick FAQs
Where do I report cybercrime in Pakistan? ▼
Report to the FIA Cyber Crime Reporting Centre (CCRC). You can file online at fia.gov.pk or visit any FIA CCRC office in person. Keep all digital evidence — screenshots, links, phone numbers — before reporting.
What law covers cybercrime in Pakistan? ▼
The Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act 2016 (PECA 2016) is the primary cybercrime law in Pakistan. It covers online harassment, hacking, digital blackmail, fake content, and cyber fraud. The FIA is the investigating authority.
What evidence to collect for cybercrime? ▼
Do NOT delete anything. Take clear screenshots of all messages, profile links, phone numbers, and URLs. Note the exact dates and times. Preserve original files. This digital evidence is critical for your FIA complaint and any court proceedings.

Quick FAQs
Minimum wage in Pakistan? ▼
The federal government notifies the minimum wage periodically under the Minimum Wages Ordinance 1961. As of 2024, the federal minimum wage is PKR 37,000 per month. Employers violating this face penalties under the Act. File a complaint at the district Labour Department — it is free.
Can I be fired without notice? ▼
Under the Industrial and Commercial Employment (Standing Orders) Ordinance 1968, termination requires one month's written notice or equivalent pay in lieu. Dismissal without valid reason or notice may be challenged before the Labour Court for reinstatement or compensation.
Working hours limit? ▼
Under the Factories Act 1934, the legal limit is 8 hours per day and 48 hours per week. Work beyond this qualifies as overtime and must be compensated at a higher rate as prescribed by law. Violations are reported to the Labour Inspector.

Quick FAQs
How to register a company? ▼
Companies are registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan (SECP) under the Companies Act 2017. You can apply online at secp.gov.pk. For a sole proprietorship or partnership, register your National Tax Number (NTN) with the Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) at fbr.gov.pk.
Is a verbal contract binding? ▼
Under the Contract Act 1872, oral agreements can be legally binding if they meet the elements of a valid contract (offer, acceptance, consideration, capacity). However, verbal contracts are very difficult to prove in court. Always use a written, signed agreement for business matters.
What to do for cheque bounce? ▼
A dishonoured cheque is a criminal offence under the Pakistan Penal Code. You may file a criminal complaint at the police station. Simultaneously, file a civil suit for recovery of the cheque amount. Consult a criminal defense lawyer for the criminal complaint and a civil lawyer for the recovery suit.
